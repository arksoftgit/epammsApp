Questions

When I type in my.enc.edu, I get a this page cannot be displayed or the Page Loading message. What should I do?

Call the ITS help desk or your Zencas System Administrator. This indicates that MyENC is not available for some reason.

 

How do I know if I should click on Main Campus or Adult Studies?

If you are working with your main campus classes and/or advisees, select Main Campus. If you are working with your adult studies classes and/or advisees, select Adult Studies.

 

What if I have Main Campus classes and Adult Studies classes, but no way to select between them when I log in?

Contact your Systems Administrator.

 

Once I am in MyENC, I cant remember whether I logged in as Main Campus or Adult Studies. How can I easily know?

Check the main MyENC banner at the top of every page. Under Faculty Academic Management there will be the words Main Campus or Adult Studies depending on how you logged in.

 

Why is there is time out and how can I work with it?

When MyENC is inactive (no command or instruction has been sent to the server) for 15 minutes, MyENC assumes that you are doing something else and will log you off for security reasons. The information to which you have access is very confidential and should your computer be left unattended, there is a chance someone else could see information they shouldnt.

The two areas where this is a problem are in entering grades or in writing emails. When entering grades for a large class, please click on Save every few minutes. This sends a command to the server letting it know you are doing something. Clicking on save does not send the grades to the Registrars office nor does it prevent you from continuing to enter grades. When writing an email, please compose the email outside of MyENC (in Word, for example). After the email is completed, copy and paste it into MyENC. Again, even though you are doing something in MyENC, nothing is sent to the server to let it know you are writing an email until you click on Send.

 

Is there a warning message?

Yes, MyENC will warn you when you have one minute left. After 14 minutes of inactivity, youll receive the message Your session will timeout in one minute. Please click OK within that time to continue and save your work if necessary.. If you immediately click OK, you will remain in MyENC, none of your work will be lost and if you continue to be inactive, the clock will reset to 15 minutes. If you do not click on OK in the minute, when you do click on OK, you will be taken to the You have been logged out screen and if you have work that needed to be saved it would be lost.

This means: How do I use the General Classes part of MyENC?

 

Note:

  1. The General Classes will display a list of all classes for a specific term, date, department, a specific schedule or active cohorts.
  2. Any column that has a small check box in the column title is sortable. Check the box and the column will sort in ascending order. Check the box again and the column will sort in descending order.

2.     Click on the down arrow in the Term box and highlight the term for which you want all classes to be listed.

3.     The default is the current term.

4.     Click on List Classes By Term.

1.     Click on the down arrow in the College box and highlight the college in which you want to do a class search (for example Undergraduate Studies, Graduate Studies, Adult Studies, Adult Studies Graduate, Certificate).

1.     Click on the down arrow in the College box and highlight the college in which you want to do a class search (for example Undergraduate Studies, Graduate Studies, Adult Studies, Adult Studies Graduate, Certificate).

3.     Click on the down arrow in the Dept box and highlight the department for which you want all classes to be listed within the pre-selected term.

4.     Click on List Classes By Department.

1.     Click on the down arrow in the College box and highlight the college in which you want to do a class search (for example Undergraduate Studies, Graduate Studies, Adult Studies, Adult Studies Graduate, Certificate).

2.     Click on the down arrow in the Term box and highlight the term for which you want all classes to be listed.

3.     Click on the down arrow in the Standard Schedule box and highlight the schedule for which you want all classes to be listed within the pre-selected term.

4.     Click on List Classes By Schedule.

  Select button to get more detail on a specific class.

  1. For more detail on a specific class, click on the Select button (far right side) for that class.
  2. You will be on the Class Detail screen for that class. The following information is displayed:

  Class number and title (Number / Title)

  Term or cohort (Term / Cohort)

  Topic name, if any (Topic)

  Number of credits (Credits)

  Class Status open, closed, cancelled (Status)

  Class Start Date

  Class End Date

  If the class fulfills a general education requirement (Gen Ed Req)

  Number of students enrolled (Enrolled)

  Number of students waitlisted (Waitlisted)

  Building and room number (Classroom)

  For every student:

o   Name of the student in alphabetical order by last name (Student)

o   Student classification - freshman, sophomore, etc. (Classification)

o   Students status in the class enrolled, waitlisted, etc. (Status)

o   If the student is taking this as a cross listed course, the cross listed course would be listed (Cross Listed Course)

  1. To go

  Back to the screen from which you came, click on Back to Classes By xxx on the left hand side.

  Back to the General Classes screen, click on General Classes in the red toolbar.

  Back to your home page, click on Home Page in the red toolbar.

Questions?
What if I do not want a grade for a student or entered a grade when I shouldnt have?
If you do not want a grade for a student, then do not enter a grade in the grade field. If you need to change a grade to no grade, use your backspace to make sure there are no characters (including blanks) in the grade field. A grade cannot be a blank character, but it can be null.

How do I know if a student is auditing this class, taking the class as honors or taking the class as pass/fail?

In the column labeled Taken As, the word audit will be displayed if the student is auditing the class and the word honors will be displayed if the student is taking the class as a honors class. If the student is taking the class pass/fail, you will not know. You need to give the grade earned and then Registrars office will convert the grade.

 

What do the error messages mean?

  The College Term is not open for the entry of midterm/final grades.
The Registrars office has not yet opened the grading period. You cannot enter any grade unless the grading period is open. The dates the grading period is open will be displayed.

  Final Grades cannot be entered because they have already been submitted.
Once you click on Submit Grades, you cannot add, change or delete a grade. Make sure you enter all grades and then click on Submit Grades. At this point you will have to contact the Registrars office to add, change or delete a grade.

  xxx Invalid table value.
xxx is the grade that you entered and is not one of the grades in the grade table. For example, if the grade F- is not in the list of valid grades, you would get this message.

  Invalid grade for student name.
This means that the grade you entered is a valid grade for a student in this class, but you are not authorized to enter this grade. For example, a W grade is valid for a student who withdraws, but only the Registrars office can give a W grade.

 

Can the Registrars Office see my grades and notes even if I didnt click on Submit Grades?
Yes, they can see anything you have entered, but until you click on Submit, they cannot move the final grades to the transcripts.

If my class is not a Pass/Fail class, but I know I have students who are taking the class Pass/Fail, should I enter an S or U?

No, you should enter the grade the student earned and Zencas will convert the grade to a S or a U.

Note:

1.     Contact History is a place that records some type of contact has been made with a person. A contact could be a phone call, a face-to-face meeting, or that an email has been sent. From within MyENC, you can record that you sent an email to a student.

The first line in the email will be "You sent the following message with contact history:" The text of your email to the students will be next.

4.     If you dont want a contact history line to be added to a students record, then you will have to decide if you want to send the email with all email addresses in the To line or send the email as a Bcc where your email address will be in the To line and the other addresses will be in the Bcc line.

5.     You cannot send an attachment AND create a contact history record. You can only use attachments from your email program, which means you cannot create a contact history record. You can get around this see the Questions section below.

  Email And Create Contact History to email the selected students and record a contact entry.

  When you do this, the date and text of your email will be included in the students Contact History.

  You cannot send an attachment if you are creating a contact entry.

  Your email program will not come up as you are sending these emails from within Zencas.

  Each email goes separately, so there is not a need for sending it as a blind email (bcc).

  You will be returned to the Email screen where you can either send additional emails or click on any of the functions to the left.

  A copy of the email will be sent to your faculty address.

  Email With No Contact History to email the selected students and not record a contact entry.

  No contact entry will be made in the students contact history.

  The email is sent from your email program so it will be brought up and you can send attachments or add other people to the email list.

3.6  Faculty Administration

3.6.1    Administration Screen

 

Functional overview: This function was created to support five situations:

  1. Even though an advisor is assigned specific advisees, in some departments, any faculty in that department can advise any student in that department. This feature will allow Division/Department Chairs to give every faculty in his/her division/department access to everybodys advisees and classes.
  2. When a faculty is on sabbatical, his/her advisees need to be temporarily assigned to someone else.
  3. In some departments, the Administrative Assistant needs access to all of the department facultys advisees and classes.
  4. Sometimes a faculty will not be able to submit grades for his/her classes and someone else in the department will need to do it for him/her.
  5. When a student is considering changing majors, sometimes a faculty in the potential major needs access to the students record to properly advisee him/her.

Notes:

  1. Only Division Chairs and Department Chairs have access to the Administration screen.
  2. Division Chairs should have access to all faculty in that division. Department Chairs should have access to all faculty in that department. If faculty are missing from the list, contact Human Resources.
  3. Division Chairs and Department Chairs can allow other people access to the advisees and classes of ONLY those faculty in their division/department. Division Chairs and Department Chairs can only remove access for those that they have assigned.
  4. Not everyone who is a Zencas user is a MyENC user. Before assigning someone access, you should confirm with them that they have or can have access to MyENC.
  5. There is no ending time for this assignment. Should the assignment need to end, the assignment will have to be removed.
  6. Faculty can see who has access to their advisees/classes by clicking on the Access To My Advisee/Classes button.
  7. Faculty Access Single is better suited for those times when you are allowing one faculty access to one facultys advisees/classes. Faculty Access Mult is better suited for when you are allowing one faculty access to a group of facultys advisees/classes OR for example, everyone in a department to everyone in the same department.
  8. When an individual student is assigned to another faculty, that faculty can only have temporary access. An ending date is required. Once that date has passed, the faculty can no longer have access unless the student is reassigned to him/her.

3.7.1    Budget Reports - MyENC

 

This means: How do I run budget reports and/or how do I enter my proposed budget from MyENC?

 

Note:

  1. Budget reports in MyENC allow for drilling down to the next level.
  2. Information in the reports is usually grouped by cost centers (departments).
  3. Information is currently displayed in a three year format:
    1. Includes information for comparing the prior year, the current year and next year using a specified fiscal year and month as the current year.
    2. Is interactive.
    3. Allows for entry of a new budget.
    4. Allows for submission of a new budget.
    5. Can be printed as a screen print. Note: This is not in a report format.
  4. Before running any report, you must select a Fiscal Year and a Month End. Note: whatever year you select is considered the Current Year for the reports.
  5. The words cost center, department and Dept Code are used interchangeably.
  6. During budget time, the column Next Budget is initialized usually to the current years budget, but can be overridden by the Controllers Office. This is more or less a default budget.
  7. New budget information is added into the requested budget column.
    ****New budget information should only be added to the Req Budget column if the number is different from the Next Budget.
  8. Use the Return button to go back to the previous screen. Please do not use the back button.

 

General Steps:

1.     Log into MyENC with your MyENC username and password.

2.     In the red tool bar line, click on Reports.

3.     Click on Budget Reports on the left. (If you do not have this button on the left, please contact the Controllers Office.)

4.     In the Time Period for Analysis (Month End and Year) box, select the Fiscal Year and the Month End. Note: Whatever Fiscal Year you select becomes the current year in the report.

5.     In the Report Structure area, click on the report that would have the account numbers for which you want to review. Reports can be for one department (cost center) or groups of departments (cost centers). For instance, you may see three reports, one for department 1, department 2 and department 3. Or you may see one report that includes department 1, 2 and 3.

6.     You should have two lines one for revenue and one for expenses for each cost center. To see the account detail for a specific cost center, click on the hyperlink in the cost center line in the Description column.

7.     To see the journal information for a specific account line, click on any hyperlink (usually displayed in blue and underlined when you point to it.) Both Current Entries and Prior Entries will be displayed.

8.     To see invoice or voucher information for a specific journal entry, click on the line item in the JE Description column. If no information exists, a message will be displayed.

9.     To return to the previous screen, click on the Return button on the left.

 

 

Entering budget numbers:

1.     Select the current fiscal year as the Fiscal Year and the month prior to the current month as the Month End.

2.     Click on the Name of the report in the Report Structure area.

3.     You should have two lines one for revenue and one for expenses for each cost center. To enter Revenue budget numbers for a specific cost center, click on the Revenue line in the Description column, to enter Expenses for a specific cost center, click on the Expenses line in the Description column.

4.     For each line item to be included in the budget,

a.      If the amount in the Next Budget column is the correct new budget amount, do not reenter the same value. Leave it blank.

b.     If the amount in the Next Budget column is not the new budget amount, click on Request Budget Change. You will be on the Set Requested Budget Amount screen. Add your requested budget amount for this account and click on Save.

5.     When you are ready to submit your budget, either click on Return to get to the Summary for Group window or drill down to the Summary for Group window. Click on Submit Budget for the Revenue or the Expense line as appropriate. You will receive a message asking if you really want to submit. If you click on Yes, your budget will be submitted and you will not be able to change your budget.

 

Questions

What if I submit my budget accidentally?

If you submit your budget accidentally, you can contact the Controllers Office. The Controller can open your budget so you to change it.

 

What do the various budget statuses mean?

Generally, the status can be:

  Blank budgets can be entered and when finished, submitted.

  Submitted budgets are closed at the department level and are ready to be reviewed by the Controllers Office.

  In Process budgets are being reviewed.

  Completed Controllers Office has finished reviewing the budget.

  Dept. Review department is to review budget again and changes budget numbers as appropriate.

 

What if I want to print any of these screens?

Click on Ctrl P to print a screen shot.
