5       Faculty Contact Lists

 

Functional overview: This function was created to allow faculty to create their own contact lists of advisees and students (in their classes). These contact lists are accessible from the Directory. Faculty can also create contact lists of prospects. These contact lists are accessible from My Admissions Prospects.

 

Examples:

1.     My Advisees: create a contact list of just freshman or just seniors.

2.     My Classes: create contact lists of groups of students working on projects.

3.     My Advisees and My Classes: create contact lists of students who are retention risks and send those lists to the appropriate ENC staff.

4.     My Admissions Prospects: create contact lists of prospects others in your department should call and send the contact lists to them.

5.     My Admissions Prospects: create a contact list of prospects to actively recruit.

6.     Directory: create a contact list of faculty on a committee.

Notes:

1.     Student (advisees or students in classes) contact lists are usable in the Directory area, NOT in My Advisees or My Classes.

2.     Prospect contact lists are usable in the My Admissions area.

3.     Faculty created contact lists are viewable and usable by only the faculty who created them unless they are sent to other users.

4.     When emailing from contact lists in the Directory, no contact history can be created.

5.     Only directory information is available about people in a contact list. To get student or class information, you must go back to those areas.

6.     For more information about contact lists in the Directory, refer to the document Directory.

 

5.1.1    Creating contact lists from My Advisees

Steps:

1.     Click on My Advisees.

2.     Your Active Advisees will be displayed.

3.     Select the advisees for whom you want to create a contact list by either

    Checking the box next to the advisees name or

    Checking the box in the title line to select all of the advisees or

    Checking the box in the title line to select all of the advisees and unchecking those you dont want.

4.     Click on Add Selected to Contact List. You will be on the Add People to Contact List screen. You can either add those people to a new contact list or an existing one. To add them to

    A new list, in the Contact List Name box, type in the name of the new contact list. Type in any Description. Click on Add People to New Contact List.

    An existing list, select the Contact List Name by clicking on the down arrow and highlighting the name of the list. You can change the description by typing in the Description box. Click on Add People to Existing Contact List.

5.     Your name(s) will be added to the created or selected list and you will be returned to the My Advisees screen.

6.     These contact lists are now accessible from the Directory function.

 

5.1.2    Creating contact lists from My Classes

Steps:

1.     Click on My Classes by Term.

2.     Your Class List will be displayed.

3.     Click on the Title of the class for which you want to create a contact list.

4.     Click on Email Students.

5.     Select the students for whom you want to create a contact list by either

    Checking the box next to the students name or

    Checking the box in the title line to select all of the students or

    Checking the box in the title line to select all of the students and unchecking those you dont want.

6.     Click on Add Selected to Contact List. You will be on the Add People to Contact List screen. You can either add those people to a new contact list or an existing one. To add them to

    A new list, in the Contact List Name box, type in the name of the new contact list. Type in any Description. Click on Add People to New Contact List.

    An existing list, select the Contact List Name by clicking on the down arrow and highlighting the name of the list. You can change the description by typing in the Description box. Click on Add People to Existing Contact List.

7.     Your name(s) will be added to the created or selected list and you will be returned to the Class Email screen.

8.     These contact lists are now accessible from the Directory function.


5.1.3    Creating contact lists from My Admissions Prospects

Steps:

1.     Click on My Admissions Prospects.

2.     You will be on the My Prospect Contact Lists screen.

3.     Select the contact list from which you want to select prospects to go on another contact list.

4.     Select the prospects for whom you want to create a contact list by either

    Checking the box next to the prospects name or

    Checking the box in the title line to select all of the prospects or

    Checking the box in the title line to select all of the prospects and unchecking those you dont want.

5.     Click on Add Selected to Contact List. You will be on the Add People to Contact List screen. You can either add those people to a new contact list or an existing one. To add them to

    A new list, in the Contact List Name box, type in the name of the new contact list. Type in any Description. Click on Add People to New Contact List.

    An existing list, select the Contact List Name by clicking on the down arrow and highlighting the name of the list. You can change the description by typing in the Description box. Click on Add People to Existing Contact List.

6.     Your name(s) will be added to the created or selected list and you will be returned to the My Prospect Contact List screen.

7.     These contact lists are now accessible from the My Admissions function.

 

 

Questions

Can I mix advisees and students from my classes and faculty into the same contact list?

Yes. When you click on Add Selected to Contact List, select an existing contact list. From within the Directory, MyENC allows you to anybody to any list.

 

Can I mix students or employees with prospects.

No. Prospects are not accessible from the Directory.

 

Why cant I create contact history if I am emailing a contact list from the Directory?

The main reason is that MyENC doesnt know where to put the contact history because you have the flexibility to mix students, advisees and employees. For example, if a student is both a student in one of your classes and one of your advisees and a student worker, MyENC doesnt know where the contact history should go.

 

 

5.2  Faculty Connections Overview

 

Functional overview: My Admissions Prospects is the online portion of Faculty Connections. Its function is to provide you with:

1.     A tool to build and shape your program through connections prospective students.

2.     Detailed information about prospects so you are better informed when you contact them.

3.     A way to communicate back to Admissions with information they can use for recruiting.

4.     A way to create and manage smaller, more customized lists of prospective students.

Notes:

1.     From time-to-time, Admissions will send you contact lists with

a.     Top recruits in your department/division,

b.     All prospects who selected a major of interest in your department/division, and/or

c.     Specific prospects that warrant your attention.

2.     These contact lists are yours. From them, you can

a.     View information about individual prospects.

b.     Create your own prospect list.

c.     Remove prospects from a contact list.

d.     Email prospects from a contact list.

e.     Add prospects to contact lists that you create.

f.      Send a contact list to another user.

g.     Delete a contact list.

3.     Prospect Notes and Contact History have different uses.

a.     Prospect Notes are for pertinent information that anyone dealing with this prospect should immediately know it is often provided to aid an ENC employee at a relational level. FOR EXAMPLE: Jared is Dr. Smiths nephew. or Susan is a National Merit Scholar.

b.     Contact History allows Admissions to maintain a record of all contact made with any given prospective student and provides Admissions with information about requests prospective students make. FOR EXAMPLE: I spoke with Susan about ENCs Engineering program and personally invited her to the High Tech ENCounter weekend. She requested a catalog.

4.     All contact should be accompanied by a Contact History entry, not all contact needs a Prospect Notes entry.

5.     Prospect Notes and Contact History are viewable by any ENC employee with access to Prospect data, in other words, notes created in MyENC are not private.

5.2.1    Using My Admissions Prospects

Steps:

1.     Click on My Admissions Prospects.

2.     Select the contact list for which you want to view prospects.

3.     Click on the name of the prospect who you are interested in.

4.     For more information on how to email prospects, how to create contact history, or how to create contact lists, refer to the document in MyENC Help My Admissions Prospects.

5.     When you contact a prospect, ALWAYS create a contact record in the Add Contact History section.

6.     If there is information that is important for anyone contacting this prospect to know, add that in the Prospect Note. DO NOT DELETE any information in the Notes. Just add to it.

7.     If you feel that Admissions should follow up with this prospect, click the Need to Follow Up box and the Save Follow Up button on the Prospect Detail screen. If the box is already checked, DO NOT uncheck it. That means someone else has checked the box and Admissions still needs to follow up.

8.     When you are done with a prospect, click on Back to Prospect List.

9.     If you would like to save this prospects name in your own contact list, check the box next to the prospect. Click on Add Selected to Contact List. For more information on working with contact lists, refer to the document in MyENC Release Notes and MyENC Help Faculty Contact Lists.

10.  When you are done with this prospect and/or added this prospect to your own contact list, check the box next to the prospects name and click on Remove Selected Members. Click on Remove & Return.

11.  When you have contacted all of the prospects in a contact list, confirm that the contact list name is displayed in the Contact List Name box and click on Delete Contact List. Click on Delete & Return.

 

Questions

Do I have to remove prospects from contact lists?

No, you can keep the prospects on the contact list, but removing them after youve made the contact might help you keep track of who is left to be contacted. However, when you receive department or division master lists, you might not want to remove names, but instead create your own contact lists of those you want to contact.

 

When I send an email, isnt a contact activity automatically recorded in contact history? Why do I have to create another one?

If you select Email And Create Contact History, a contact activity is recorded in contact history and you dont have to create another entry. However, if for some reason you either selected Blind Email With No Contact History or sent an email from outside MyENC, you should create a contact history with a Type of Email.

 

When would I use the Send List to User feature?

You might want to send a contact list to other faculty in your department for either their review or for them to follow up. You might want to send a contact list to Admissions with specific notes.

9.1  Faculty Administration

9.1.1    Administration Screen

 

Functional overview: This function was created to support five situations:

  1. Even though an advisor is assigned specific advisees, in some departments, any faculty in that department can advise any student in that department. This feature will allow Division/Department Chairs to give every faculty in his/her division/department access to everybodys advisees and classes.
  2. When a faculty is on sabbatical, his/her advisees need to be temporarily assigned to someone else.
  3. In some departments, the Administrative Assistant needs access to all of the department facultys advisees and classes.
  4. Sometimes a faculty will not be able to submit grades for his/her classes and someone else in the department will need to do it for him/her.
  5. When a student is considering changing majors, sometimes a faculty in the potential major needs access to the students record to properly advisee him/her.

Notes:

6.     Only Division Chairs and Department Chairs have access to the Administration screen.

7.     Division Chairs should have access to all faculty in that division. Department Chairs should have access to all faculty in that department. If faculty are missing from the list, contact Human Resources.

8.     Division Chairs and Department Chairs can allow other people access to the advisees and classes of ONLY those faculty in their division/department. Division Chairs and Department Chairs can only remove access for those that they have assigned.

9.     Not everyone who is a Zencas user is a MyENC user. Before assigning someone access, you should confirm with them that they have or can have access to MyENC.

10.  There is no ending time for this assignment. Should the assignment need to end, the assignment will have to be removed.

11.  Faculty can see who has access to their advisees/classes by clicking on the Access To My Advisee/Classes button.

12.  Faculty Access Single is better suited for those times when you are allowing one faculty access to one facultys advisees/classes. Faculty Access Mult is better suited for when you are allowing one faculty access to a group of facultys advisees/classes OR for example, everyone in a department to everyone in the same department.

13.  When an individual student is assigned to another faculty, that faculty can only have temporary access. An ending date is required. Once that date has passed, the faculty can no longer have access unless the student is reassigned to him/her.
